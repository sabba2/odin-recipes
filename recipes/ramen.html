Japanese Ramen

Ramen s a Japanese noodle dish. It consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a broth; common flavors are soy sauce and miso, with typical toppings including sliced pork hāshū, nori (dried seaweed), menma (bamboo shoots), and scallions. Ramen has its roots in Chinese noodle dishes. Nearly every region in Japan has its own variation of ramen, such as the tonkotsu (pork bone broth) ramen of Kyushu and the miso ramen of Hokkaido.

The following recipe is a simplified version that uses chicken, noodles, spinach, sweetcorn and eggs to make this moreish Japanese noodle soup, for when you crave something comforting yet light and wholesome.

Ingredients

  • 700ml chicken stock
  • 3 garlic cloves, halved
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce, plus extra to season
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice
  • pinch of chilli powder
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 375g ramen noodles
  • 400g sliced cooked pork or chicken breast
  • 2 tsp sesame oil

For the garnish

  • 100g baby spinach
  • 4 tbsp sweetcorn
  • 4 boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 1 sheet dried nori, finely shredded
  • sliced green spring onions or shallots
  • sprinkle of sesame seeds
